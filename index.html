FEVA

Hello! My name is Silvia Ferrer. Welcome to my portfolio!


Feva comes from two simple latin roots: Silvia is connected to silva, the Latin word for forest, which points to things that grow, change, and spread in chaotically different directions. Ferrer comes from ferrum, iron, which is a symbol of structure and strength. Put together, Feva fits a portfolio that is built on clear academic foundations but still leaves room to explore, adapt, and try new things.


In this portfolio, I have created web pages for some of the projects I have worked on in recent years.

Each project has a title and a small description. Please click any title to see more.

Projects

  • CO2 Emissions and Temperature

    This research project examines the different types of CO2 emissions and how they have trended across countries over the past 300 years, as well as their impact on temperature increases.

    It was crafted using Tableau's analytic and graphic tools -> Prep, Desktop, Public

  • SICSS Project: EPP Thesis

    During the Summer Institute of Computational Social Science (SICSS) at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU) in 2025 (organized by the CMU Libraries) I had the honor to be part of the SICSS Sense team for the final research project: we looked into some trends among the dissertations of the PhD students of the Engineering and Public Policy Department (EPP) of CMU.
    For the presentation, we explained our research journey and all the challenges we faced using Joseph Campbell's The Journey of the Hero process.

    We used the College's repository to download the raw data, and R to clean, analyse, and graph it.

  • Drug and Opioid Overdose Data Interactive Report in Racine County

    During my internship at the Public Health Division of Racine County, Wisconsin, I analyzed data on suspected overdoses among Racine County residents, reported by emergency department visits between 2020 and 2023.
    I used OpenRefine to clean data, and Power BI to digest it and create the report.
